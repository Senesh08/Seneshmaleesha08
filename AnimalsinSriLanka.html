Indigenous Animals In Sri Lanka

Sri Lankan Sloth Bear


Another big animal found in the nation is the Sri Lankan sloth bear, which coexists with the Sri Lankan leopard for the most part of its habitat. It is an omnivore bear that lives in lowland dry woods and eats flesh and carrion in addition to berries, roots, and nuts.With less than 1,000 specimens remaining (and a wild population of roughly 500), this bear is very vulnerable. The primary causes of that include habitat degradation (this bear heavily depends on natural forests for food supplies), persecution following livestock losses, property damage, and assaults on people. It is therefore feared in Sri Lanka.
